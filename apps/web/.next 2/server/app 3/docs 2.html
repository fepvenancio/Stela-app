The Stela Codex

Everything you need to understand, integrate, and build on the Stela P2P lending protocol.

What is Stela?

Stela is a peer-to-peer lending protocol on StarkNet. No liquidity pools, no oracles, no intermediaries — just direct agreements between borrowers and lenders.

Every loan is an inscription — an immutable on-chain record with its own isolated Collateral Locker contract. Lenders receive ERC1155 Shares as transferable proof of their position.

The protocol supports ERC20, ERC721, ERC1155, and ERC4626 assets for debt, interest, and collateral — from stablecoins to NFTs.

Quick Start

Get started with Stela in three steps. No deposits into pools, no complex setup — just sign and go.

1

Connect Your Wallet

Connect a StarkNet wallet — either Argent or Braavos. Click the connect button in the top right corner of any page.

2

Create a Borrow Order

Go to the Borrow page. Fill in what you want to borrow (debt), what you will put up as collateral, and the interest you are offering to the lender. Set a duration and deadline, then sign the order with your wallet.

3

Wait for a Lender

Your signed order appears on the Markets page. When a lender fills your order, a relayer bot settles it on-chain — locking your collateral, transferring the debt to you, and minting ERC1155 shares for the lender. No extra action needed from you.

Atomic Swaps

Use the Swap page for atomic peer-to-peer trades. Assets exchange instantly in a single transaction with no repayment period and a lower fee (0.15% vs 0.25%).

Core Terminology

Collateral

The Guarantee. Assets provided by the borrower and locked in a secure per-inscription Locker contract. If the borrower defaults, these assets are forfeit to the lender.

Debt

The Principal. The exact assets and amounts the borrower wishes to receive. Lenders provide these assets to "seal" the inscription.

Interest

The Reward. The additional assets the borrower agrees to pay the lender on top of the debt principal upon repayment. Interest is pro-rated by elapsed time on early repayment — borrowers only pay for the time they held the debt.

Deadline

The Discovery Period. A unix timestamp defining when the inscription stops accepting lenders. If reached without a signature, the inscription expires and collateral can be reclaimed.

Inscription Lifecycle

Every inscription follows a clear sequence of state transitions, from creation to final redemption.

I

Inscribe (Open)

The borrower locks collateral into a dedicated Locker contract and defines the terms: debt requested, interest offered, collateral, and duration.

  • Collateral
  • Debt
  • Interest
  • Deadline
II

Fund (Filled)

A lender signs the inscription, providing the debt assets. The borrower receives liquidity immediately. The lender receives ERC1155 shares as proof of their position. The repayment clock starts now.

III

Repay (Repaid)

The borrower returns the debt plus interest before the duration expires. Collateral is released. Shareholders can now redeem. Interest is pro-rated by elapsed time — early repayment means lower interest cost. The formula uses ceil(interest × elapsed / duration), rounding up to protect lenders.

IV

Liquidate (Liquidated)

If the borrower fails to repay before the duration expires, anyone can call liquidate(). The collateral becomes claimable by shareholders proportionally.

V

Redeem

After repayment or liquidation, shareholders burn their ERC1155 tokens to claim a proportional share of the underlying assets.

Other States

!
Expired

If the deadline passes without a signature, the inscription expires. No debt was ever issued, so no assets are at risk. The borrower can safely cancel and reclaim their collateral.

X
Cancelled

Borrowers may cancel an open inscription at any time before it is signed. The locker releases the collateral, and the Stela is permanently deactivated.

Core Mechanics

Shares (ERC1155)

Stela issues ERC1155 Share Tokens for every loan. These tokens are transferable receipts of your lending position. Whoever holds the shares at the time of settlement holds the right to claim the underlying assets.

Percentage Precision

The protocol operates with 0.01% precision (1/10,000). Interest rates and multi-lender contributions are all calculated with this granularity to ensure maximum accuracy without floating-point errors.

Multi-Lender Mode

Inscriptions can be "crowdfunded." Multiple lenders can sign a single Stela, each providing a percentage of the total debt. The ritual remainsPartial until 100% of the debt is covered.

Collateral Lockers

Stela uses a Factory Pattern to deploy a fresh Locker Contract for every inscription. This unique architecture ensures that your collateral is never pooled with others, providing absolute isolation.

Atomic Swaps (Duration = 0)

Head to the Swap page to create an atomic swap. Both sides of the trade execute in a single transaction — assets swap atomically with no repayment period, no interest assets required, and no liquidation risk.

Swaps pay a reduced fee of 0.15% (vs 0.25% for loans), split between the relayer (0.05%) and treasury (0.10%).

Swap Use Cases

  • OTC trades — large token swaps without slippage or AMM price impact
  • Multi-asset swaps — trade bundles of tokens in a single transaction
  • NFT trades — swap ERC721 or ERC1155 tokens peer-to-peer
  • Cross-standard swaps — exchange ERC20 for ERC721, or any combination of asset types

Off-Chain Signing

Borrowers create orders by signing SNIP-12 typed data — no gas required. When a lender matches, a relayer bot settles the order on-chain.

I

Sign Order (Off-Chain)

The borrower signs a SNIP-12 InscriptionOrder typed data message with their wallet. This contains all loan terms — collateral, debt, interest, duration, deadline, and a nonce for replay protection. No transaction is sent, so no gas is paid.

SNIP-12 Typed Data

StarkNet's typed data standard (like EIP-712 on Ethereum). The wallet shows the user exactly what they are signing — asset types, amounts, and terms — before they approve.

II

Submit Offer (Off-Chain)

A lender signs a SNIP-12 LendOffer specifying the order ID and the percentage of debt they want to provide. The signed offer is stored off-chain alongside the order.

III

Bot Settlement (On-Chain)

When an order is fully matched (offers total 100%), the relayer bot calls settle() on the Stela contract with both signatures. The contract verifies each signature on-chain via SNIP-12 typed data hashing, then executes the loan — locking collateral, transferring debt, and minting shares.

IV

On-Chain Verification

The settle() entrypoint reconstructs the SNIP-12 type hashes for both InscriptionOrder and LendOffer, verifies signatures against the signer accounts, consumes nonces, and executes the inscription creation and signing atomically.

Order Book Model

Borrowers can post multiple orders with different terms. Lenders browse and choose which to fund — creating a peer-to-peer order book.

Compete on Terms

Post multiple orders with different rates, collateral ratios, or durations. Lenders pick the terms they prefer — the market decides which fills.

First Fill Wins

All orders from the same borrower share a nonce. When one settles on-chain and consumes the nonce, all sibling orders are automatically expired — like cancel-on-fill limit orders.

No Double Settlement

The on-chain NoncesComponent enforces strict equality — even if multiple orders exist, only one can ever settle. The contract guarantees this cryptographically.

Genesis NFT Discounts

NFT Fee Discounts

Genesis NFT holders receive automatic protocol fee discounts. Holding 1+ NFT grants a 15% base discount, plus +5% per volume tier (7 tiers from $10K to $1M+) and +2% per additional NFT, capped at 50% off. Discounts apply to the treasury portion of fees only — the 0.05% relayer fee is never discounted. Applied on-chain by reading the holder's NFT balance — no claiming or staking required. Treasury NFTs (IDs 1-50) are held by the protocol. Public minters (IDs 51-300) purchase at 1,000 STRK each.

Discount Floors

Even at the maximum 50% discount, the treasury always receives a minimum fee. For lending settlements, the treasury floor is 10 BPS (0.10%). For swaps, the treasury floor is 5 BPS (0.05%). The relayer's 5 BPS is never discounted.

Volume Tiers

Your cumulative settlement volume is tracked per-address on-chain (only whitelisted tokens count toward volume). Each tier adds +5% to your discount: $10K, $25K, $50K, $100K, $250K, $500K, and $1M+.

Treasury & Governance

No Admin

Ownership has been permanently renounced on all deployed contracts. There is no multisig, no governance, no backdoor.

No Upgrades

Contracts are immutable. No pause function, no proxy pattern, no parameter changes. The code is the protocol.

Open Access

Every protocol function — settle, liquidate, redeem, repay, cancel — is callable by anyone. No whitelists, no gatekeepers.

Treasury Reserve

50 of 300 Genesis NFTs are minted to the protocol treasury at deployment. This is hardcoded in the constructor — not an admin action. Treasury NFTs are held for protocol development: security audits, ecosystem growth, and operational costs.

Per-Wallet Limit

Public minting is capped at 5 NFTs per wallet, enforced on-chain via the MAX_PER_WALLET constant. No single participant can accumulate an outsized fee discount.

Ownership Renounced

After deployment, contract ownership is permanently renounced via OpenZeppelin's renounce_ownership(). No admin functions can ever be called again — mint price, mint status, base URI, and admin minting are all permanently locked.

Risks & Limitations

Stela has been audited, but all DeFi protocols carry inherent risks. Understand these before using the protocol.

Smart Contract Risk

The Stela contracts have been audited, but no audit can guarantee the absence of bugs. Smart contracts are immutable once deployed — if a vulnerability is discovered, the affected contract cannot be patched.

Counterparty Risk

Stela uses time-based liquidation, not price-based. If collateral value drops below debt value during the loan period, the lender receives the depreciated collateral upon liquidation. There is no automatic margin call or early liquidation trigger.

No Price-Based Liquidation

This is by design — Stela uses no oracles, which eliminates oracle manipulation risk. However, it means lenders must independently evaluate collateral quality and volatility. The protocol does not protect against collateral depreciation during the loan term.

Flash-Loan NFT Gaming

A user could theoretically flash-loan a Genesis NFT to receive a fee discount, then return it in the same transaction. This is an accepted low risk on StarkNet due to the transaction structure making such attacks impractical compared to other chains.

Fee-on-Transfer Tokens

Tokens that charge fees on transfer (deflationary tokens, tax tokens) may cause accounting discrepancies. The contract records the nominal transfer amount, but fewer tokens may actually arrive. Use standard ERC20 tokens for best results.

Rounding Dust

Very small amounts (less than 1 wei) may be lost to integer rounding in share calculations and proportional distributions. This is inherent to integer arithmetic on-chain and affects negligible amounts only.

Knowledge Base

What happens if no one lends to my inscription?

The inscription will eventually hit its deadline and expire. No debt was ever provided, so your collateral remains safe. You can reclaim it by cancelling the expired Stela.

Can I cancel after a lender has signed?

No. Signature creates an immutable on-chain debt obligation. You must repay the debt plus pro-rated interest within the duration to release your collateral.

Is there an oracle or price feed?

No. Stela is oracle-free. It does not liquidate based on price fluctuations, only on time. This eliminates oracle manipulation risks.

Can I create multiple orders at once?

Yes. You can post several orders with different terms simultaneously — like limit orders on an exchange. Lenders choose which to fund. When one settles, all others from the same nonce are automatically cancelled. Only one can ever fill.

Are shares transferable?

Yes. Every lending position is an ERC1155 token. You can transfer your shares to another address, and they will hold the right to claim the assets.

Do I pay full interest if I repay early?

No. Interest is pro-rated by elapsed time using ceil(interest x elapsed / duration). Repaying a 30-day loan at day 15 costs roughly half the interest. Rounding is ceiling (up) so lenders always receive at least 1 wei. Swaps (duration = 0) always charge full interest.

Architecture

Stela is a fully on-chain protocol with off-chain indexing for discovery. All state transitions happen through direct smart contract interactions — the backend never proxies writes.

Smart Contracts (Cairo)

Core protocol logic, inscription state machine, locker deployment, share minting. Written in Cairo for StarkNet.

Indexer (Cloudflare Worker)

Polls StarkNet RPC for contract events and writes to D1 (SQLite). Enables browsing and discovery of inscriptions.

Frontend (Next.js)

Server-rendered UI deployed on Cloudflare. Reads from D1 for listing, connects directly to StarkNet for writes via user wallet.

Liquidation Bot (Cloudflare Worker)

Automated cron job that monitors for expired inscriptions and executes liquidations on-chain.
